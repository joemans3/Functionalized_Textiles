The Science

How Stimulus-Responsive Hydrogels Work

Hydrogels are crosslinked polymer networks that swell or contract depending on their environment. By engineering the polymer chemistry, we program the mesh to respond precisely to chosen stimuli.

When a trigger is detected, the polymer chains undergo a conformational change — expanding or collapsing the hydrogel matrix and physically altering the pore size of the mesh structure. This process is fast, reversible, and repeatable over thousands of cycles.
